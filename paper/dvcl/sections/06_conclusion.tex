\section{Conclusion}

In this paper, we proposed DVCL, a dual-view contrastive framework for robust heterogeneous graph neural networks. DVCL combines a purified semantic topology view with a feature-induced view and aligns their node representations through a symmetric cross-view contrastive objective. This design preserves heterogeneous semantic information while reducing exclusive dependence on a potentially corrupted graph topology.

The experimental framework uses accuracy as its sole metric and enforces identical data splits, attack artifacts, target nodes, random seeds, and tuning rules across methods. Under the current unified ACM protocol, preliminary evidence shows that DVCL is more accurate than our HSeCo reproduction under PRBCD, HetePRBCD, and the FGSM-style structural target attack. This conclusion is intentionally limited: the final paper will require audited HSeCo checkpoints, multiple complete datasets, and external baselines such as RoHe and HeteGuard before making a broader state-of-the-art claim.

The present evaluation focuses on structural perturbations. Since DVCL also relies on a feature-induced view, feature attacks and adaptive attacks that jointly target topology and attributes are necessary to characterize its full robustness boundary. Future work may further extend DVCL to dynamic heterogeneous graphs and other attack surfaces.
